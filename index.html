🕯️ PAGE 2 — Candle Lighting

点烛仪式

English

Leader: “God brings light into the world and into our lives.”

All: “Jesus is our Light.”

John 8:12 (KJV)
“Then spake Jesus again unto them, saying, I am the light of the world: he that followeth me shall not walk in darkness, but shall have the light of life.”

How to lead:

  1. Dim the room lights.
  2. Light two candles.
  3. Pause for a moment of quiet gratitude.
  4. Say the opening lines together.

Short Prayer: Lord Jesus, thank You for bringing light, truth, and hope into our home tonight. Amen.

John 1:5 (KJV)
“And the light shineth in darkness; and the darkness comprehended it not.”

Illustration: Two candles with a soft glow, with a child helping an adult.

中文

领袖：“上帝将光带进世界，也带进我们的生命。”

全体：“耶稣是我们的光。”

约翰福音 8:12（中文意译）
“耶稣又对众人说：我是世界的光；跟从我的，就不在黑暗里走，必要得着生命的光。”

带领方式：

  1. 将房间灯光调暗。
  2. 点燃两支蜡烛。
  3. 安静片刻，向主献上感谢。
  4. 一起朗读开场回应词。

简短祷告：主耶稣，感谢祢将光、真理和盼望带进我们的家。阿们。

约翰福音 1:5（中文意译）
“光照在黑暗里，黑暗却不能胜过光。”

插图说明： 两支温柔发光的蜡烛，孩子与大人一同点烛。

🍷 PAGE 3 — Cup 1: Sanctification

第一杯：成圣之杯

English

Leader: “God sets us apart as His people.”

All: “We belong to God.”

Exodus 6:6–7 (KJV)
“I am the LORD, and I will bring you out from under the burdens of the Egyptians… And I will take you to me for a people, and I will be to you a God.”

Meaning: This first cup remembers that God calls His people out and makes them His own. As Christians, we remember that we are holy because we belong to Christ.

Blessing: Blessed are You, O Lord our God, King of the universe, who calls us to be Your people and gives us new life in Christ.

Action: Lift the cup. Pause. Drink together.

1 Peter 2:9 (KJV)
“Ye are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, an holy nation, a peculiar people...”

Host note: Grape juice works well for families.

中文

领袖：“上帝将我们分别出来，成为祂的子民。”

全体：“我们属于上帝。”

出埃及记 6:6–7（中文意译）
“我是耶和华；我要把你们从埃及人的重担下领出来……我要以你们为我的百姓，我也要作你们的上帝。”

意义： 第一杯记念上帝呼召祂的百姓出来，并使他们归属于祂。对基督徒而言，我们也记念自己因着基督被分别为圣。

祝福词： 宇宙的主，我们的上帝，当受称颂；祢呼召我们作祢的子民，并在基督里赐给我们新生命。

动作： 举起杯，安静片刻，然后一同饮用。

彼得前书 2:9（中文意译）
“惟有你们是被拣选的族类，是有君尊的祭司，是圣洁的国度，是属上帝的子民……”

主办提示： 家庭聚会可用葡萄汁代替酒。

🍽️ PAGE 4 — Tasting the Symbols

品尝象征之物

English

Leader: "Let's taste and remember. Each food on this plate tells a part of the story."

1. Karpas (Parsley in Salt Water)

Host: Lifts the parsley. "This green reminds us of new life in springtime. But we dip it in salt water to remember the tears of slavery."

Psalm 126:5 (KJV)
"They that sow in tears shall reap in joy."

All: Dip parsley in salt water and eat.

2. Maror (Bitter Herbs)

Host: "Now we taste the bitterness of slavery."

Exodus 1:14 (KJV)
"And they made their lives bitter with hard bondage..."

All: Eat a small amount of the bitter herb.

3. Charoset (Fruit & Nut Paste)

Host: "This reminds us of the mortar for bricks, but its sweetness speaks of the hope of freedom."

All: Eat the Charoset, often with a piece of matzah.

4. Zeroa (Roasted Bone) & Beitzah (Egg)

Host: "The lamb bone reminds us of the Passover lamb, whose blood protected God's people. The egg reminds us of new life and the festival sacrifice."

1 Corinthians 5:7 (KJV)
"For even Christ our passover is sacrificed for us."

Note: We look at these but do not eat them now. The egg can be eaten with the meal.

中文

领袖： “让我们来品尝和记念。盘中的每一样食物都在诉说一部分故事。”

1. Karpas (浸盐水的香菜)

带领人： 举起香菜。“这绿色让我们想起春天的新生命。但我们把它浸在盐水里，是为了记念为奴的泪水。”

诗篇 126:5（中文意译）
“流泪撒种的，必欢呼收割。”

全体： 将香菜浸入盐水后食用。

2. Maror (苦菜)

带领人： “现在我们来品尝为奴的苦涩。”

出埃及记 1:14（中文意译）
“他们因服苦役，生活就觉得愁苦……”

全体： 食用少量苦菜。

3. Charoset (果仁泥)

带领人： “这让我们想起砌砖的泥，但它的甘甜诉说着自由的盼望。”

全体： 食用果仁泥，通常会搭配一小块无酵饼。

4. Zeroa (烤骨) & Beitzah (烤蛋)

带领人： “羊骨让我们想起逾越节的羔羊，它的血保护了上帝的子民。鸡蛋则提醒我们新的生命和节期的献祭。”

哥林多前书 5:7（中文意译）
“因为我们的逾越节羔羊基督，已经为我们被献上了。”

注： 我们现在只看不吃。鸡蛋可以在正餐时食用。

✝️ PAGE 7 — The Last Supper & The Cross

最后的晚餐与十字架

English

Host: “Jesus shared a Passover meal with His disciples. During that meal, He took the bread and cup and showed that the story of rescue was pointing to Him.”

Matthew 26:26–28 (KJV)
“Jesus took bread, and blessed it, and brake it, and gave it to the disciples... And he took the cup... saying... this is my blood of the new testament, which is shed for many for the remission of sins.”

Gentle words for children:

Jesus went to the cross because He loves us very much. He felt real pain and sadness, but He chose to give Himself for us so we could be brought close to God and forgiven.

Meaning for friends who are new to faith:

The Bible teaches that sin breaks our relationship with a holy God. Jesus did not die because He had done wrong, but because we had. On the cross He took our place, bearing suffering and judgment, so that we could be justified—made right with God by grace, not by our own goodness.

Isaiah 53:5 (KJV)
“But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed.”
Romans 5:8–9 (KJV)
“But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us... being now justified by his blood, we shall be saved from wrath through him.”

中文

带领人：“耶稣与门徒所吃的最后晚餐，就是逾越节晚餐。在那一餐中，祂拿起饼和杯，显明这整个拯救的故事最终都指向祂自己。”

马太福音 26:26–28（中文意译）
“耶稣拿起饼来，祝福，就掰开，递给门徒……又拿起杯来……说：这是我立约的血，为多人流出来，使罪得赦。”

对孩子温柔的说明：

耶稣上十字架，是因为祂非常爱我们。祂真实地经历了痛苦和忧伤，但祂甘愿这样做，为要把我们带回上帝面前，使我们得赦免。

对未信主朋友的意义：

圣经说，罪使我们与圣洁的上帝隔绝。耶稣受死，不是因为祂做错了什么，而是因为我们有罪。祂在十字架上代替我们承担痛苦与审判，好让我们因着恩典被称为义，不是靠自己的好行为，乃是靠祂为我们所成就的。

以赛亚书 53:5（中文意译）
“哪知祂为我们的过犯受害，为我们的罪孽压伤；因祂受的刑罚我们得平安，因祂受的鞭伤我们得医治。”
罗马书 5:8–9（中文意译）
“惟有基督在我们还作罪人的时候为我们死，上帝的爱就在此向我们显明了……现在我们既靠着祂的血称义，就更要借着祂免去上帝的忿怒。”

🍞 PAGE 8 — Breaking the Matzah & Afikomen

掰无酵饼与 Afikomen

English

Leader: “After remembering Jesus at the Passover table, we now break the matzah as He broke the bread and gave thanks. This broken bread reminds us of His body, given for us.”

Luke 22:19 (KJV)
“And he took bread, and gave thanks, and brake it, and gave unto them, saying, This is my body which is given for you: this do in remembrance of me.”

How to do it:

  1. Hold up the matzah and thank God for His provision.
  2. Break it in two pieces.
  3. Wrap one piece in a napkin and hide it as the Afikomen.
  4. Keep the other piece to share later.

Afikomen Search: After the meal, children search for the hidden piece. The finder may receive a small prize or lead the next blessing.

Afikomen explained — origin & meaning: The word likely comes from the Greek ἀφικόμενον (afikomenon), meaning “that which comes after” (an after‑meal portion). Jewish and Aramaic forms (often spelled afikoman) appear in later tradition; the term refers to a matzah portion set aside and eaten later. Symbolically, the Afikomen is a broken and hidden piece of matzah that children search for: it makes the Seder playful and participatory, recalls the Passover meal, points forward (for Christians) to Christ’s broken body and coming redemption, and creates a moment of joyful anticipation and intergenerational connection.

John 6:35 (KJV)
“I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger...”

中文

领袖：“当我们刚刚记念耶稣在逾越节餐桌上的话语之后，现在我们也像祂那样掰开无酵饼、献上感谢。这被掰开的饼使我们想起祂为我们舍去的身体。”

路加福音 22:19（中文意译）
“祂拿起饼来，祝谢了，就掰开，递给他们，说：这是为你们舍的我的身体；你们也应当如此行，为的是记念我。”

进行方式：

  1. 举起无酵饼，为上帝的供应献上感谢。
  2. 把饼掰成两半。
  3. 将其中一半用餐巾包起来，藏起来，作为 Afikomen。
  4. 另一半留待稍后分给大家。

寻找 Afikomen： 饭后让孩子们寻找藏起来的那一块。找到的人可以得到一个小奖品，或带领下一段祝福。

Afikomen 说明与词源： 该词可能源自希腊语 ἀφικόμενον（afikomenon），意为“随后而来之物”（饭后之份）；在晚期犹太/阿拉米语传统中也有类似写法（afikoman）。象征上，Afikomen 是一块被掰开、包好并藏起来的无酵饼：它让逾越节既好玩又具参与性，提醒我们逾越节的餐点意义；对基督徒而言，它也指向基督被掰开的身体与将来要成全的救赎，同时成为家庭中传承与期待的时刻。

约翰福音 6:35（中文意译）
“我就是生命的粮；到我这里来的，必定不饿……”

🍷 PAGE 12 — Cup 4: Praise & Cup 5: Hope

第四杯：赞美与第五杯：盼望

English

Leader: “We praise God for His promises.”

All: “God keeps His promises!”

Psalm 107:1 (KJV)
“O give thanks unto the LORD, for he is good: for his mercy endureth for ever.”
Psalm 113:2–3 (KJV)
“Blessed be the name of the LORD from this time forth and for evermore. From the rising of the sun unto the going down of the same the LORD'S name is to be praised.”

Response Prayer: Lord, You are faithful. Thank You for keeping every promise and completing salvation through Jesus.

Action: Lift the fourth cup and praise God together.

A Note on Elijah's Cup & The Cup of Christ:

In many Seders, a cup is poured for the prophet Elijah, who is expected to return and announce the Messiah. As Christians, we believe the Messiah has already come. Jesus Himself is our hope and our redemption. This final cup is for Him, the one who promises, "Behold, I am with you always."

Glossary of Key Terms

关键词汇解释

Matzah (无酵饼): Unleavened flatbread, reminding us of the haste of the Exodus.

Seder (宴仪): The ritual meal with a set order of readings, blessings, and symbolic foods.

Haggadah (礼仪书): The book or script used at the Seder.

Chametz (有酵食物): Leavened foods forbidden during Passover.

Seder Plate (象征盘): A plate holding symbolic items for the story.

Karpas (绿菜): A green vegetable (like parsley) dipped in salt water to recall tears of slavery.

Maror (苦菜): Bitter herbs to remember the bitterness of slavery.

Charoset (果仁泥): A sweet paste symbolizing the mortar used by the Israelites.

Zeroa (烤羊骨): A roasted bone symbolizing the Paschal lamb.

Beitzah (烤蛋): A roasted egg symbolizing sacrifice and new life.

Four Cups (四杯酒): Four cups of wine representing God’s four promises of redemption. Many traditions add a fifth cup symbolizing our hope for the coming kingdom.

Kiddush (祝酒词): The blessing over wine that sanctifies the meal.

Afikomen (阿菲可门): A broken piece of matzah that is hidden and then found, which for Christians beautifully foreshadows Christ’s death and resurrection.

🎭 APPENDIX A2 — Family Story Skit

English

Characters: Narrator, Moses, Pharaoh, People (can be one or more).

Props (Optional): A staff for Moses, a crown for Pharaoh, blue cloth for the Red Sea.

Narrator: The Israelites were slaves in Egypt, building cities for Pharaoh. They cried to God for help.

People: (Sounding tired) “Lord, save us! This is too hard.”

Narrator: God chose Moses to lead them. Moses went to Pharaoh.

Moses: (Boldly) “The God of Israel says, ‘Let my people go!’”

Pharaoh: (Stubbornly) “No! I will not let them go. They must work harder!”

Narrator: God sent ten plagues. After the final, terrible plague, Pharaoh’s heart was broken.

Pharaoh: (Sadly) “Go! Leave my land. Take your people and serve your God.”

Narrator: But soon, Pharaoh changed his mind and chased them to the Red Sea!

People: (Scared) “We are trapped! The army is behind us and the sea is in front!”

Moses: “Do not be afraid. Stand firm and you will see the deliverance the LORD will bring you today.”

Narrator: Moses lifted his staff, and God parted the sea. The people walked through on dry ground.

All: (Joyfully) “God has rescued His people! He is our strength and our song!”
